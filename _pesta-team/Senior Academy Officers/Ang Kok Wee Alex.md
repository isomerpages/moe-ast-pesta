<h5>Kok Wee Ang (Alex) (Senior Academy Officer)</h5>
<p></p>

</div>
<p>Prior to joining PESTA, Alex taught at Northbrooks Secondary School for
12 years. During this time, Alex worked closely with student leaders and
played a key role in developing the school’s Learn for Life Programme (LLP)
in Outdoor Education, serving as Subject Head of Student Leadership &amp;
Outdoor Education for four years. In recognition of contributions to student
leadership and outdoor education, Alex was awarded the Outstanding Youth
in Education Award (OYEA) in 2020. Alex also led the team in conceptualising
the inaugural National Orienteering Race (Schools) and forged a partnership
with Mandai Wildlife Reserves to develop a Zoo Orienteering Race, which
received the Innergy Bronze Award in 2021.</p>
<p>Alex subsequently took on the role of Head of Department for Physical
Education and Co-Curricular Activities for three years. In this capacity,
Alex charted the professional learning journey of the PE team, focusing
on e-pedagogy and the development of 21st Century Competencies (21CC).
These efforts culminated in opportunities to share the team’s work at both
local and international conferences. Alex later pursued a Master of Science
in Outdoor Education at the University of Edinburgh, marking the next chapter
of the professional journey before joining PESTA.</p>
<p>Alex is a strong advocate for experiential learning, grounded in the belief
that meaningful learning endures over time. Alex’s work consistently promotes
student ownership, agency, and advocacy, aligned with the philosophy of
“challenge by choice.” At the organisational level, Alex values inter-disciplinary
thinking and synergistic partnerships to create rich learning experiences.
Such collaborations provide authentic, real-world contexts that foster
global awareness and cross-cultural literacy among students.</p>
<p>In the domains of Physical Education, sports, and outdoor learning, Alex
believes these platforms are powerful in developing students’ critical
and adaptive thinking across a variety of contexts. Through sport, students
cultivate values such as integrity, teamwork, and sportsmanship that extend
beyond the playing field. Alex also emphasises the role of the natural
environment in shaping students’ appreciation of local landscapes. The
skills and dispositions developed through outdoor experiences serve as
catalysts for environmental stewardship and conservation.</p>
